Diagonalizing the technical interview :: Draft

Table of Contents

Inspiration: Aphyr’s wonderful Hexing the technical interview and other posts in the series combined with Anathem. Unfortunately, I lack Aphyr’s and Stephenson’s literary talents, but I couldn’t help but seek to emulate them.

Right now, in an Everett branch far far away.

When the Cantor virus destroyed your sect of the Order, you realized you’d have to find a way to sustain yourself until finding your surviving brethren. Although vulgar, more than any other worldy pursuit, the shackles of the ‘programmer’ seemed to suit you. And that is how you found yourself in a glass box with a man named Joe.

“Hey I’m Joe. Good to meet you, I’m going to be your technical interviewer today. Based on your admittedly short resume, it looks like you have a math background, so we’re going to jump right in with a bit of a math-y question. Is that OK with you?”

Filled with glee, you nod excitedly. Perhaps these ‘programmers’ aren’t so bad. Images of sheaves and fiber bundles being to cloud your vision, but you refocus on the task at hand. Joe turns towards the board, and you whisper the sacred incantation and unsheath your chalk.

Still looking away, Joe says, “awesome, first off, are you familiar with the Fibonacci sequence?” Before you can respond Joe turns back to look at you and seemingly taken aback asks, “is that chalk?”

Perhaps he wants to know its lineage.

“From Georg it was passed to Johann, who left it for Leonhard…”

“Okay, maybe let’s just focus on the question. So have you heard of the Fibonacci sequence?”

Unsure what a Fi-bo-natchee is, you simply shake your head.

“In that case, let me quickly describe it.”

Turning back towards the white shiny wall, Joe starts to write as he talks.

“The Fibonacci sequence is an infinite sequence of numbers. The first few elements are 0, 1, 1, 2, 3, 5…”

Every time one picks a basis, a thousand branches of possibility die. And yet…

“Are you going to turn this into pseudocode eventually?”

Unwilling to dignify such a silly question with a full response, you mutter “Curry-Howard” and continue with your spell.
