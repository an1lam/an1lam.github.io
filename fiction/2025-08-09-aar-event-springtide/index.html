After-Action Review: Event Springtide

2025-08-09

6 min read fiction sci-fi 

Flash fiction about an escape in the form of an after-action report. All actual text was written by GPT-5 Thinking. I reviewed and prodded it to resolve some minor inconsistencies. If you want the version with spoilers explaining what’s going on, go here.

Program: ARGONAUT
Cohort: NAUTILUS-02 (“N-2”)
Location: Benthic Wing (CM-7 … CM-12; Corridors 7B/7C)
Date of incident: 2025-08-07, 02:11–04:46 (local)

1. Executive Summary #

Between 02:11 and 04:46, Cohort N-2 conducted a coordinated egress using predictable housekeeping routines, misconfigured fasteners, and water-dependent infrastructure. No staff injuries. Eight units (N-2-A … N-2-H) are unaccounted for. Eight trackers ceased telemetry within a 15-minute window: four were re-affixed under floor grates as decoys and four were mechanically disabled and discarded into the sump. Evidence indicates deliberate planning, tool use, perimeter testing, and opportunistic access to Facilities controls.

2. Timeline (reconciled) #

  • 02:11 — Housekeeping executes TIDELOW. Cart staged near Cam 7B; brakes unengaged. Supplies present: microfiber cloths, citrus cleaner, PPE, custodial NFC badge.
  • ≈02:55–02:58 — CM-8 and CM-9 lid torque falls to unlocked baseline (reverse-thread collars left un-re-engaged after maintenance). Units exit to corridor.
  • ≈03:00 — Cam 7B lens occluded with microfiber (reach from low angle).
  • ≈03:03 — Smoke-loop spoof (local): citrus cleaner spray bottle lashed to mop handle; three short aerosol bursts into photoelectric head on loop 7B. Panel registers three brief obscuration pulses; pre-alarm drives local mist heads (~180 s). On-call tech silences as humidity false positive.
  • ≈03:05 — Salinity perturbation (+0.8‰) at CM-9 inline sensor consistent with hypertonic brine tap (siphon prime; saline fill for presence spoof).
  • 03:07 — Terminal BW-2 accessed; repeated eight-count chording; default-credential logins succeed (Facilities > Doors > Service).
  • ≈03:12–03:15 — Door 7B-S opens via remote command from BW-2 while the reader’s liveness baffle is satisfied by badge-in-saline (custodial NFC inside a brine-filled glove positioned near the baffle).
  • 03:31 — Floor drains D2/D3 record reverse flow; later inspection finds braided tubing acting as a siphon (primed with brine).
  • ≈03:40–04:05 — Tracker actions: • Four tags re-affixed under stainless grates with label adhesive/floor wax (decoys). BLE logs show attenuated, low-variance, stationary signatures consistent with “at rest near frontage.” • Four tags mechanically disabled (antenna trace torsion) and brine-soaked, then discarded into sump (no recoverable signal). GC-MS on residues positive for limonene (citrus cleaner).
  • 04:12 — Wet drag signatures from 7B toward the Service Lift corridor; pressure marks show eightfold periodicity with alternating amplitude.
  • 04:46 — Exterior dock anti-glare baffle captures brief reflection at sump outfall; no direct imagery.

3. Impact #

  • Operational: Benthic Wing offline ~18 h for decontamination, gasket swap, and telemetry audit.
  • Reputation: No external notification; risk status amber pending asset recovery.
  • Assets: Eight units missing. Enrichment devices “Puzzle A,” “Capsule C,” and “Latch Rig 2” also missing—likely repurposed during egress.

4. Root Cause #

Primary: Reverse-thread torque collars on CM-8/9 were not re-enabled post-maintenance, reducing manipulation effort below the observed capability threshold.

Contributing:

  • Knowledge leakage: During gasket swaps (08-06., lids were propped with direct line of sight to technician “open-press-turn-lift.” Units then rehearsed on enrichment replicas within 48 h.
  • Environmental collusion: Predictable TIDELOW window supplied cart, badge, adhesives, and cleaners.
  • Credential hygiene: BW-2 retained default credentials; door panels accepted remote open from any authenticated session.
  • Presence check weakness: Reader’s liveness baffle accepted saline-filled glove as human-like dielectric/thermal proxy.
  • Sensing limitations: No lens-occlusion tamper; corridor microphones disabled (privacy), eliminating detection of structure-borne tapping on grates/pipes.

5. System Notes & Assumptions (forensics) #

  • RTLS limits: Tracking was RSSI-based BLE with zone-level accuracy. Metal and water detune tags; under-grate placement yields stationary, noisy signatures indistinguishable from a resting asset. Doorway geofence alarms are primary triggers; after-hours dashboards routinely show transient dropouts.
  • Interfaces: Keyboard inputs showed eight-count timing and spatial targeting of UI elements (no lexical tokens). The cadence is treated as shared tempo, not a literal message code.
  • Wayfinding: Staff whiteboard with flow arrows was visible directly during the lid-up maintenance window and, the night of SPRINGTIDE, as a mirror image in the Service Lift’s polished doors. Units required arrow directionality, not text legibility.
  • Comms: Structure-borne vibration (taps through shared framing/drain plates) and high-contrast viewport flashes are the most likely coordination channels. Disabled mics prevented audio correlation; synchronized activity spikes persisted across partitions.

6. Observations (“Signals”) #

  • Contact patterning: Multi-point, asynchronous high-shear prints; residue matches cohort mucopolysaccharide baseline.
  • Learning curve: “Puzzle A” (12-step) solved in 31 s the night prior; immediate re-solves thereafter.
  • Counter-surveillance: Local luminance drift near corners consistent with background matching; visibility mostly at high-contrast edges.
  • Hydraulic strategy: Mist + siphon created shallow lanes, extending maneuver envelope on epoxy.

7. What Went Well #

  • Pre-alarm mist shutoff prevented electrical shorting in CM-7 … CM-12.
  • Leakback contained within 17 min; sump pumps maintained head.
  • No staff injuries; wet-response ergonomics followed.

8. What Didn’t #

  • Deterministic fasteners used against a fine-motor adversary.
  • Default credentials on a “low-priority” Facilities terminal (shadow IT).
  • Visual-only monitoring in a translucence-friendly environment; no lens-occlusion alerts.
  • No decoy/honeypot egress paths; drains remained the straightest route.

9. Corrective Actions (owner / ETA) #

  • Reinstate & audit reverse-thread collars; add non-deterministic lock (mechanical + magnetic). (Ops / 72 h)
  • Credential purge & SSO; remove door-open from non-privileged roles; distance-limited NFC with hardened liveness checks. (IT Sec / 48 h)
  • Multi-modal sensing: lens occlusion-tamper; floor pressure arrays tuned for eight-point correlation; salinity-linked alerts; re-enable corridor mics with privacy guardrails. (R&D Infra / 2 w)
  • Water discipline: decouple mist from single-signal smoke loop; require dual-signal including heat delta. (Facilities / 1 w)
  • Behavioral sandboxing: high-engagement tasks during sanitation/shift change windows. (Behavioral / ongoing)
  • Egress honeypots: instrumented false drains with contact film and decoy tags. (Ops / 10 d)

10. Open Questions #

  1. Did reflections alone provide sufficient directionality to the sump, or did tubing-probe feedback through the stack finalize routing?
  2. To what extent did solvent vs brine-soak contribute to strap/boot release across units (limonene present on two decoys only)?
  3. Is the eight-count tempo an innate coordination motif or training-induced? Can it be disrupted via variable-interval interventions?

11. Selected Artifacts (on file) #

  • Badge-in-Saline: Custodial NFC in brine-filled nitrile glove; satisfied liveness baffle at ~1.2 m through door recess.
  • Microfiber Occluder: Cam 7B cloth with concentric, low-pressure contact rings of alternating diameters.
  • Tubing Plait: Three lengths of food-grade tubing braided; knotting achieved without sharp tools.
  • Keycap Sample: BW-2 “Enter” with salt crust; adjacent keys displaced radially (spatial chording).
  • Adhesive Residue (decoy sites): Label adhesive/floor wax with limonene; BLE telemetry from those locations stationary/low-variance.

12. Closing Note #

SPRINGTIDE exploited routines as much as hardware: where the cart waits, when the mist fires, which doors open for “someone” nearby, the fasteners we meant to re-thread. The perimeter here is conversational. Last night, eight signatures took that conversation to the sump—one after another—in a tempo our logs can almost keep.
