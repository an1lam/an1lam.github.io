Linear Algebra Done Right - Chapter 3

Posted on March 30, 2019

Selected Exercises 3.D 7. Suppose $ V $ & $ W $ are finite-dimensional. Let $ v \in V $. Let $$ E = \{ T \in \mathcal L(V, W): T(v) = 0 \}. $$ (a) Show that $ E $ is a subspace of $ \mathcal L(V, W) $. (b) Suppose $ v \neq 0 $. What is $\dim E$? For (a), to show $ E $ is a subspace of $ \mathcal L(V, W) $, we need to show that $ E $ contains zero and is closed under both addition and multiplication. [Read More]
